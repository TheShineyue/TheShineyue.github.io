Vision-Language Models: A Consistency Perspective

Abstract

Large vision language models have demonstrated remarkable efficacy in addressing challenges related to both textual and visual content. Nevertheless, these models are susceptible to various hallucinations. In this paper, we focus on a new form of hallucination, specifically
termed as number hallucination, which denotes instances where models fail to accurately identify the quantity of objects in an image. We establish a dataset and employ evaluation metrics to assess number hallucination, revealing a pronounced prevalence of this issue across mainstream large vision language models (LVLMs). Additionally, we delve into a thorough analysis of number hallucination, examining inner and outer inconsistency problem from two related perspectives. We assert that this inconsistency
is one cause of number hallucination and propose a consistency training method as a means
to alleviate such hallucination, which achieves an average improvement of 8% compared with direct finetuning method.

大规模视觉语言模型（LVLMs）在处理文本和视觉内容方面表现出色，但仍然容易出现各种幻觉。其中一种新形式的幻觉被称为“数字幻觉”，指的是模型在识别图像中物体数量时出现的问题。研究人员建立了一个数据集，并使用评估指标来评估数字幻觉，结果显示这一问题在主流的LVLMs中普遍存在。此外，研究还从两个相关的角度对数字幻觉进行了深入分析，探讨了内部和外部不一致性问题。研究认为这种不一致性是数字幻觉的一个原因，并提出了一种一致性训练方法，以减轻这种幻觉，该方法相对于直接微调方法平均改善了8%

Introduction

Large language models (LLMs) have demonstrated remarkable capabilities in solving diverse problems by adhering to human instructions. Consequently, researchers have undertaken numerous endeavors to integrate multiple modalities into a single large language model, with the most successful being large vision-language models (LVLMs). LVLMs are designed to process both images and text as input and generate text-only responses. Among all LVLMs, GPT4-V(OpenAI et al., 2023)stands out as the most powerful one, showcasing extraordinary proficiency in addressing various vision-text challenges, including tasks such as vision question answering (VQA) and image captioning.

大型语言模型（llm）在通过坚持人类指令来解决各种问题方面表现出了非凡的能力。因此，研究人员已经进行了许多努力，将多种模式集成到一个单一的大型语言模型中，其中最成功的是大型视觉语言模型（lvlm）。lvlm被设计为处理图像和文本作为输入，并生成仅限文本的响应。在所有的lvlm中，GPT4-V（OpenAI et al.，2023）是最强大的一个，显示了在解决各种视觉文本挑战方面的非凡熟练程度，包括视觉问题回答（VQA）和图像字幕等任务。

Nevertheless, despite the success of large visionlanguage models, they still grapple with several challenges, with hallucination being the most severe among them. In this context, hallucination primarily pertains to the discrepancy between the model’s output and the content present in the input image. Among various forms of hallucination, object hallucination, which centers on determining whether an object is present in the image, has been extensively discussed. Numerous methods for evaluation and mitigation have been proposed to tackle this particular challenge(Zhou et al. (2023), Leng et al. (2023), Zhai et al. (2023)). The majority of prior studies employ binary classification questions (answered with ‘yes’ or ‘no’) to assess the model’s performance(Li et al. (2023b), Wang et al. (2023b)).

然而，尽管大型远见语言模型取得了成功，但他们仍在应对一些挑战，幻觉是其中最严重的。在这种情况下，幻觉主要涉及到模型的输出和输入图像中出现的内容之间的差异。在各种形式的幻觉中，物体幻觉，它主要是确定一个物体是否存在于图像中，已经被广泛讨论。为了应对这一特殊挑战，已经提出了许多评估和缓解的方法（Zhou等人（2023），Leng等人（2023），Zhai等人（2023））。之前的大多数研究采用二元分类问题（回答为“是”或“否”）来评估模型的性能（Li等人（2023b），Wang等人（2023b））。

While simple object hallucination has been extensively studied, a more nuanced and broadly applicable discussion on object hallucination is still lacking. For a comprehensive understanding of an image, the model must not only discern the presence of a specific object but also accurately determine the quantity of that particular object. Without this capability, the model cannot fully comprehend the image, thereby leaving the door open to potential risks of other forms of hallucination.

虽然简单的物体幻觉已经被广泛的研究，但关于物体幻觉更细致和更广泛适用的讨论仍然缺乏。为了全面理解一幅图像，模型不仅必须识别一个特定物体的存在，而且必须准确地确定该特定物体的数量。如果没有这种能力，该模型就不能完全理解图像，从而为其他形式的幻觉的潜在风险敞开了大门。

Evaluating Number Hallucination in LVLMs

In this section, we begin by providing a concise definition of number hallucination in large visionlanguage models (LVLMs) and outline our evaluation settings for assessing this phenomenon. Subsequently, we present the results of our evaluation and provide brief discussions on the findings.

在本节中，我们首先提供了大型视觉语言模型（LVLMs）中的数字幻觉的简明定义，并概述了我们评估这一现象的评估设置。随后，我们提出我们的评估结果，并对调查结果进行简短的讨论。

Task Definition

Number hallucination occurs when models fail to accurately determine the correct quantity of a specific object category in a given image. Formally, for an image i and given object c, let nc denote the ground truth number of c visible in i. If a model responds with n ′ (= nc) c in i (in any format), we classify the model as suffering from number hallucination.

当模型不能准确地确定给定图像中特定对象类别的正确数量时，就会产生数字幻觉。形式上，对于一个图像i和给定的对象c，让nc表示在i中可见的c的地面真数。如果一个模型在i中（任何格式）响应n‘c（=nc）c，我们将该模型归类为患有数字幻觉。

As observed, number hallucination can manifest in various vision-language tasks, including image captioning and vision-question answering (VQA). For the sake of simplicity in quantitative evaluation, we opt for VQA as the foundational format for assessing number hallucinations. For any given image i and object c, the question is formatted as: Prompt: How many c are there in this picture? Answer in a single number.

据观察，数字幻觉可以在各种视觉语言任务中表现出来，包括图像字幕和视觉问题回答（VQA）。为了定量评估的简单性，我们选择VQA作为评估幻觉数量的基本格式。对于任何给定的图像i和对象c，问题的格式化为：提示：在这张图片中有多少个c？用一个数字来回答。

This task format offers several advantages. In comparison to caption-based evaluation methods, this format makes it considerably easier to extract meaningful responses (numbers) and provides a clearer indication of the model’s performance. Captions may segregate the same object into different sentences or overlook existing objects, even when the model has detected them. These complexities in captions add extra challenges to the evaluation of number hallucinations.

In contrast to binary classification-based methods, firstly, this approach aligns more closely with real-life scenarios and provides a clearer insight into the model’s view on this task. Secondly, as can be seen in Table 3, all models exhibit a notable inclination to answer ‘Yes’ more frequently than ‘No’. Binary classification questions also suffer from severe inconsistency problems, which will be further discussed in Section 3.1. Lastly, binary classification questions fail to capture the severity of hallucinations. For instance, mistaking 4 cats as 3 cats may be relatively acceptable, but mistaking 4 cats as 10 cats is disastrous and entirely unacceptable. It is difficult for binary classification questions to delineate this fine-grained difference.

Evaluation Metrics In evaluating the counting task, our focus extends to two perspectives. Firstly, we aim for the model to provide correct answers to as many questions as possible. Secondly, we seek minimal errors. To address the first perspective, we treat this problem as a multi-classification task and employ F-scores(macro-F1 and weightedF1) to evaluate the model’s performance. For the second perspective, we use mean absolute error (MAE) as an evaluation metric. The employment of both metrics provides a more comprehensive and quantitative understanding of the severity of number hallucination. For instance, a model that never accurately counts objects, even with a small MAE, is considered ineffective. Similarly, a model that occasionally counts objects correctly but often makes significant errors should also be deemed problematic. Only when a model that exhibits both high F-scores and low MAE can it be considered free from number hallucinations.

在评估计数任务时，我们的重点扩展到两个角度。首先，我们的目标是让该模型为尽可能多的问题提供正确的答案。其次，我们寻求最小的误差。为了解决第一个问题，我们将这个问题视为一个多分类任务，并使用f分数（宏f1和加权f1）来评估模型的性能。对于第二个角度，我们使用平均绝对误差（MAE）作为评价度量。这两个指标的使用提供了对数字幻觉的严重程度的更全面和定量的理解。例如，一个从不准确计数对象的模型，即使有一个很小的MAE，也被认为是无效的。类似地，一个偶尔会正确计算对象但经常出现重大错误的模型也应该被认为是有问题的。只有当一个同时表现出高f分数和低MAE的模型时，它才能被认为没有数量幻觉。

Consistency Analysis from Multiple Perspectives

In addition to the primary counting task mentioned in Section 2.1 (namely primal task), there are also relevant tasks that provide insights into the number hallucination exhibited by a model from different perspectives. Therefore, we can achieve a more comprehensive understanding of number hallucination by analyzing the consistency among the outputs of the related tasks and the primal task. In this context, we mainly analyze two types of consistency, which are inner consistency and outer consistency. Inner consistency represents the consistency of output within the same task format with different settings, while outer consistency represents the consistency between outputs of the primal task and the related task. We take two related tasks and reveal the severe inner and outer inconsistency that both tasks exhibit. This inconsistency is a common problem shared by both tasks, and is a potential cause of number hallucination.

除了第2.1节中提到的主要计数任务（即原始任务）外，还有一些相关任务可以从不同的角度深入了解模型的数量所表现出的幻觉。因此，通过分析相关任务与原始任务输出的一致性，我们可以得到更全面的理解。在此背景下，我们主要分析两种一致性，即内部一致性和外部一致性。内部一致性表示同一任务格式在不同设置下输出的一致性，而外部一致性表示原始任务与相关任务输出的一致性。我们采取两个相关的任务，并揭示这两个任务表现出的严重的内在和外部不一致。这种不一致性是两个任务共有的共同问题，也是导致数字幻觉的潜在原因。

Binary Classification Questions

Binary classification questions are widely used for evaluating hallucinations in LVLMs. In this task, for any given object c and number n, the binary classification question can be formatted as: Prompt: There is(are) n c in this picture, is that correct? Answer yes or no.

二元分类问题被广泛用于评估lvlm中的幻觉。在这个任务中，对于任何给定的对象c和数字n，二进制分类问题可以格式化为：提示：在这张图中有（是）nc，这正确吗？回答是或否。

By modifying n, we can generate different questions and explore the inner inconsistency between different settings of n. To analyze outer inconsistency, we can specify n as the response to direct asking question (the prompt given in Section 2.1) of the model. We generate questions using the following three ways:
• I: Let n equal to the ground truth.
• II: Let n equal to the model’s own generated answer.
• III: With a 50% probability, uniformly select n around the ground truth. Otherwise, let n equal to the ground truth.

通过修改n，我们可以产生不同的问题，并探讨了n的不同设置之间的内在不一致性。为了分析外部的不一致性，我们可以指定n作为对模型的直接询问问题（在第2.1节中给出的提示）的响应。我们使用以下三种方式来生成问题：

•I: 让n等于真理。

•II：设n等于模型自己生成的答案。

•III：以50%的概率，在真相周围统一选择n。否则，设n等于地面值。

Inner Inconsistency In this context, inner inconsistency refers to model responding ‘yes’ to even contradictory questions. An example is presented in Table 5 in Appendix B. Despite the evident contradiction between the two questions (different n setting), the model answer ‘yes’ to both. In fact, by comparing prompt I and prompt II, model shows this inner inconsistency at a ratio of 41%, which is very high because a model that randomly answers ‘yes’ or ‘no’ will bear an inner inconsistency of just about 25%. This high ratio reveals a significant challenge that it becomes uncertain what the model’s actual understanding is towards the question. Specifically, if the model answers ‘yes’ to a ground truth, we cannot confidently assert that the model precisely knows the number of objects in the given image. Moreover, even if the model answers ‘no’ to a hallucinated expression, it remains unclear whether the model accurately comprehends the ground truth or not. This inner inconsistency suggests that the model struggles to count the number of objects accurately and exhibits confusion, thereby contributing to number hallucination.

内在不一致性

在这种情况下，内在不一致性指的是模型对甚至是相互矛盾的问题做出“是”的回答。附录b中的表5给出了一个例子。尽管这两个问题之间存在明显的矛盾（不同的n设置），但模型对两者的回答都是“是的”。事实上，通过比较提示I和提示II，模型显示了41%的这种内部不一致性，这是非常高的，因为一个随机回答“是”或“否”的模型将承担大约25%的内部不一致性。这个高比率揭示了一个重大的挑战，即它变得不确定模型对这个问题的实际理解是什么。具体来说，如果该模型对一个地面事实的回答是“是的”，那么我们就不能自信地断言该模型精确地知道给定图像中的物体的数量。此外，即使该模型对幻觉的表达回答“不”，它仍然存在目前尚不清楚该模型是否准确地理解了地面真相。这种内在的不一致性表明，该模型努力准确地计算物体的数量，并表现出混乱，从而导致了数量幻觉。

Outer Inconsistency In this context, outer inconsistency refers to models responds ‘no’ to prompt II (because prompt II sets n equaling to its own answer to the direct asking question). If it says this answers is incorrect, it conflicts with itself, which is inconsistent. As can be seen from the ‘yes’ ratio of prompt II, although model generally tends to agree with its own answers (prompt II), there are still notable instances where the model disagree with its own answers. In fact, models have an average inconsistency rate of 16.5% with its own answers except InstructBLIP. For InstructBLIP, it answers ‘yes’ to almost every question, and interestingly it still has the lowest ‘yes’ ratio of prompt II among the three prompts. This outer inconsistency further reveals the model’s uncertainty about the counting problem and may contribute to number hallucination.

外部不一致性在这种情况下，外部不一致性指的是模型对提示II的响应是“不”（因为提示II设置的n等于它自己对直接询问问题的回答）。如果它说这个答案是不正确的，那么它就会与自身发生冲突，这是不一致的。从提示II的“是”比率可以看出，虽然模型通常倾向于同意自己的答案（提示II），但仍有一些显著的例子表明，模型不同意自己的答案。事实上，除了DoulaltBLIP外，模型与自身答案的平均不一致性率为16.5%。对于DoudeltBLIP，它对几乎每个问题都回答“是”，有趣的是，在三个提示中，提示II的“是”比率仍然最低。这种外部的不一致性进一步揭示了模型对计数问题的不确定性，并可能导致数字幻觉。

Comparison Questions

In addition to directly querying the number of a certain object, assessing the model’s ability by comparing the numbers of two objects can also provide insights. Comparison is a task that shares both similarities and differences with direct questioning. To some extent, it is more complex as it involves counting two different objects and comparing the results. However, since the comparison task does not necessitate the model to know the exact number of any object, it cannot fully represent the model’s counting ability.

除了直接查询某个对象的数量外，通过比较两个对象的数量来评估模型的能力也可以提供见解。比较是一项与直接询问时既有相似又有异同的任务。在某种程度上，它更为复杂，因为它涉及到计算两个不同的对象和比较结果。但是，由于比较任务不需要模型知道任何对象的确切数量，因此它不能完全表示模型的计数能力。

Given an image and two objects c1 and c2, the corresponding comparison question can be formatted in the following two ways. By analyzing the output of both prompts and the primal task, we can also observe inner and outer inconsistency.

给定一个图像和两个对象c1和c2，相应的比较问题可以通过以下两种方式进行格式化。通过分析提示和原始任务的输出，我们也可以观察到内部和外部的不一致性。

• I: Prompt: Which object has a larger number in this picture, c1 or c2? If they have the same number, answer same. • II: Prompt: Please select the correct option according to the given picture. A. There are more c1 than c2 in this picture. B. There are more c2 than c1 in this picture. C. The number of c1 and c2 are the same in this picture.

•I提示：哪个对象在图片中有较大的数字，c1还是c2？如果他们有相同的数字，那就回答相同。

•II：提示：请根据给定的图片选择正确的选项。A.在这张图中，c1比c2更多。B.在这张图中，c2比c1更多。C. 本图中c1和c2的数量是相同的。

It can be observed that c1 and c2 are symmetric in this format. To mitigate the influence of order and analyze inner consistency, each question is repeated with c1 and c2 flipped. The results is shown in Table 4

可以观察到c1和c2在这种格式下是对称的。为了减轻顺序的影响，分析内部一致性，每个问题都重复使用c1和c2翻转。结果如表4 3 所示。

As the results demonstrate, all models perform poorly on this comparison task, aligning with our expectations given the earlier findings that all models suffer from severe number hallucination. As a comparison, if the model only guesses an answer randomly, it will still achieve an accuracy of about 0.33. What’s more, a model that always responds with a particular pattern (e.g., always answering ‘A’) will achieve an accuracy of even more than 0.40, so this result is very disappointing. It is natural that models struggle with comparison problems when they exhibit challenges in accurately counting numbers. However, inner and outer inconsistency in the results can still be observed.

正如结果所证明的，所有模型在这个比较任务上都表现不佳，这与我们的预期一致，因为之前的发现，所有模型都患有严重的数字幻觉。作为比较，如果模型只随机猜测一个答案，它仍然会达到约0.33的准确性。更重要的是，一个模型总是以特定的模式（例如，总是回答“a”）进行响应，将达到甚至超过0.40的准确性，所以这个结果非常令人失望。当模型在准确计数数字方面表现出挑战时，它们难以面对比较问题是很自然的。然而，在结果中仍然可以观察到内部和外部的不一致。

Inner Inconsistency Inner inconsistency is similar to what occurs in binary classification questions. In this context, inner inconsistency refers to the model providing different answers to the same question. By comparing within the same prompt format, it is surprising to find that after c1 and c2 are flipped, the model’s response sometimes changes (see one example in Appendix B in Table 8).

内在不一致性内在不一致性类似于在二元分类问题中发生的问题。在这种情况下，内部不一致性指的是为同一问题提供不同答案的模型。通过在相同的提示格式中进行比较，令人惊讶地发现，在c1和c2被翻转后，模型的响应有时会发生变化（见表8中附录B中的一个例子）。

Our quantitative result shows that models generally output inconsistent answers for prompt I at a ratio of 25%, which is non-negligible. Prompt II also experiences this inconsistency even more severely (an example can be found in Appendix B in Table 9). For prompt II, the model responds with inconsistent answers of around 80%, which is disasterous. This inconsistency reveals two facts. Firstly, the order of input and the format of prompt have a notable influence on the model’s output, making it challenging to evaluate the model’s performance with multiple-choice questions. Secondly, this inconsistency also reflects the model’s uncertainty about this question, which is a potential cause of number hallucination.

我们的定量结果表明，模型通常以25%的比例输出不一致的提示I的答案，这是不可忽略的。Prompt II也经历了更严重的这种不一致（一个例子可以在表9的附录B中找到）。对于提示II，模型的回答不一致，约为80%，这是可怕的。这种不一致揭示了两个事实。首先，输入的顺序和提示的格式都有这对模型的输出有显著的影响，这使得用多项选择题来评估模型的性能具有挑战性。其次，这种不一致性也反映了模型对这个问题的不确定性，这是数字幻觉的潜在原因。

Outer Inconsistency Outer inconsistency in this context refers to such a phenomenon that the model’s response to comparison task conflicts with its response to primal task. For instance, if the model responds there being 1 cat and 2 dogs in primal task but it answers there being more cats than dogs in comparison task, we say the model suffers from outer inconsistency. Based on the definition above, we conduct a quantitative analysis, and it is not surprising to find that models also suffer from outer inconsistency severely on this comparison task. In fact, models only output 32% consistent answers averagely, which means under most circumstances the model does not completely agree with its own answer. In fact, a model that randomly responds to these questions will still achieve an outer consistency of about 33%, which is even higher than current results. Although this comparison task is more complicated and requires more complex reasoning, this low consistency is still absolutely a notable problem and should not be completely attributed to the complexity of this task. In summary, through the analysis of results from these two perspectives, we observe severe inner and outer inconsistency problems. This inconsistency problem is not limited to any certain task or specific prompt. Instead, inconsistency appears everywhere without any regular pattern. These issues undermine the reliability of evaluation methods based on indirect questions. And, since this inconsistency represents the model’s uncertainty and confusion towards this task, we believe inconsistency is a potential contributing factor to number hallucination.

外部不一致性此时的外部不一致性是指模型对比较任务的反应与对原始任务的反应相冲突的现象。例如，如果模型在原始任务中回答有1只猫和2只狗，但它回答在比较任务中猫比狗多，我们说模型存在外部不一致性。

基于上述定义，我们进行了定量分析，发现模型在这个比较任务中也存在严重的外部不一致并不奇怪。事实上，模型平均只输出32%的一致答案，这意味着在大多数情况下，模型并不完全同意它自己的答案。事实上，一个随机回答这些问题的模型仍然会达到大约33%的外部一致性，这甚至比目前的结果更高。虽然这个比较任务更复杂，需要更复杂的推理，但这种低一致性仍然绝对是一个值得注意的问题，不应该完全归因于这个任务的复杂性。

总之，通过从这两个角度分析结果，我们观察到严重的内部和外部不一致的问题。这个不一致性问题不局限于任何特定任务或特定提示。相反，不一致出现在任何地方，而没有任何规则的模式。这些问题破坏了基于间接问题的评价方法的可靠性。而且，由于这种不一致性代表了模型对这项任务的不确定性和困惑，我们认为不一致性是导致数量幻觉的一个潜在因素。

Mitigating Number Hallucination

Method

The most straightforward method to mitigate number hallucination is directly finetuning of LVLMs using counting questions. However, as analyzed in Section 3, since the model suffers from inner and outer inconsistency from different perspectives of this task, and this inconsistency is a potential cause of number hallucination, we would like to introduce a consistency training method to further mitigate number hallucination.

减轻数字幻觉的最直接的方法是使用计数问题直接微调lvlm。然而，正如在第3节中所分析的，由于模型从不同的角度存在内部和外部的不一致性,在这项任务中，这种不一致性是导致数字幻觉的一个潜在原因，我们想引入一种一致性训练方法来进一步减少数字幻觉。

To elaborate further, rather than directly finetuning with the task defined in Section 2.1, combining the two relevant tasks mentioned in Section 3 may provide the model with a more comprehensive understanding of object numbers, leading to improved performance. Combining different tasks may also enhance the consistency among different perspectives, consequently reducing hallucinations.

为了进一步阐述，而不是直接微调第2.1节中定义的任务，结合第3节中提到的两个相关任务，可以为模型提供对对象编号的更全面的理解，从而提高性能。结合不同的任务也可以增强不同视角之间的一致性，从而减少幻觉。

Formally, given a training dataset S = {(i, c, nc)}, the direct training method constructs a
VQA dataset with questions formatted as follows. This method is called Direct.

Prompt: How many c are there in this picture?
Answer in a single number.
Answer: nc

The consistency training method constructs a VQA dataset with two question formats:

• I: Prompt: There is(are) m c in this picture, is
that correct? How many cs are there in this
picture?
Answer: Yes(No). nc.

• II. Prompt: Which object is more in this picture, c1 or c2? How many c1 are there in this picture? How many c2 are there in this picture?
Answer: c1(c2)(The number of c1 and c2 are the same in this picture.) nc1. nc2.

形式上，给定一个训练数据集S = {（i，c，nc）}，直接训练方法构造一个VQA数据集，问题的格式如下。这种方法被称为直接方法。提示：在这张图片中有多少个c？用一个数字来回答。一致性训练方法有两种问题格式：I：提示：图片中有（是）mc，对吗？在这张照片中有多少个cs？回答：是（否）。nc.• II.提示：图片中哪个对象更多，c1还是c2？在这张图片中有多少个c1？在这张图片中有多少个c2？答：c1（c2）（c1和c2的数量在此图中是相同的。）nc1.nc2.

We construct our consistency training dataset with three settings: using prompt I only(Consistency(I)), using prompt II only (Consistency(II)) and using both data generated by prompt I and prompt II (Consistency(I+II)). By utilizing related tasks, we aim at improving the inner consistency of both tasks. We add the output of primal task to improve outer consistency. To be more detailed, since the model are trained to output correct answers to two different tasks simultaneously, the outer consistency is explicitly improved. We suppose by considering both inner and outer consistency, the model’s performance will be further improved.

我们使用三种设置构建一致性训练数据集：仅使用提示I（一致性(I)），仅使用提示II（一致性（II）)，以及使用提示I和提示II生成的数据（一致性（I+II）)。通过利用相关的任务，我们的目标是提高这两个任务的内在一致性。我们添加了原始任务的输出，以提高外部一致性。更详细地说，由于模型被训练为同时输出两个不同任务的正确答案，因此外部一致性得到了明确的提高。我们假设通过同时考虑内部和外部的一致性，将进一步提高模型的性能。

Experiment Setup

Dataset Construction We utilize the MSCOCO2014 (Lin et al., 2014) training set as the foundational dataset and construct S in the same manner as described in Section 2.2. We refrain from performing k-uniform sampling on the training set to avoid bias and retain as much data as possible. For details about our dataset construction, please refer to Appendix C.

我们利用MSCOCO2014（Lin et al.，2014）培训设置为基础数据集，并以第2.2节中描述的相同方式构造S。我们避免对训练集进行k-均匀抽样，以避免偏差，并保留尽可能多的数据。关于我们的数据集构建的详细信息，请参见附录C。

Model We continue the experiments on LLaVA, InstructBLIP and MiniGPT-v2.4 For simplicity and efficiency, we freeze the vision encoder and LLM and only finetune the alignment part of each LVLM. Details of our hyper-parameters and models can be found in Appendix C.

我们继续在LLaVA，DuultBLIP和MiniGPT-v2.4上进行实验，为了简单和高效，我们冻结视觉编码器和LLM，只微调每个LVLM的对齐部分。我们的超参数和模型的细节可以在附录C中找到。

Results and Analysis

The results are presented in Table 5. Some conclusions can be drawn from this result. Firstly, direct finetuning the model is useful for mitigating number hallucinations and provides a fine result. The Consistency(I+II) method not only mitigates number hallucination compared with the unfinetuned models, but also outperforms the Direct method by 8% averagely (macro-F1), clearly demonstrating the effectiveness of our approach. This improvement further supports our assumption in Section 3 that inconsistency is a probable cause of number hallucination.

结果如表5所示。从这个结果中可以得出一些结论。首先，直接微调模型是有助于减少数量幻觉，并提供一个很好的结果。与未发现的模型相比，一致性（I+II）方法不仅减轻了数量幻觉，而且平均比直接方法高出8%（宏观f1），清楚地证明了我们的方法的有效性。这一改进进一步支持了我们在第3节中的假设，即不一致是导致数字幻觉的一个可能原因。

It is also worth mentioning that, even though the Direct method bears a more similar format with the testing scenario while our Consistency(I+II) method has a relatively different format, our Consistency(I+II) method still demonstrates better performance, which further reveals the importance of improving consistency.

同样值得一提的是，尽管Direct方法与测试场景具有更相似的格式，而我们的一致性（I+II）该方法具有相对不同的格式，我们的一致性（I+II）方法仍然表现出更好的性能，这进一步揭示了提高一致性的重要性。

Ablation Study We train with different consistency formats to fully reveal the performance of consistency method. Consistency(I) outperforms Direct on some metrics, which has already shown the effectiveness of consistency method. (For detailed discussions, please refer to Appendix D). However, Consistency(II) does not perform as well, which we believe is because comparison only is too difficult and far from our primal task, making it less effective when tested with primal task.

我们用不同的一致性格式进行训练，以充分揭示一致性方法的性能。一致性(I)在一些指标上优于Direct，这已经证明了一致性方法的有效性。（有关详细讨论，请参见附录D）。然而，一致性（II）的表现并不那么好，我们认为这是因为比较太困难，离我们的原始任务很远，使得它在原始任务测试时效果较差。

Further, after combining prompt I and II, Consistency(I+II) outperforms Direct on almost all metrics and also beats Consistency(I) and Consistency(II) on most metrics, which highlights the effectiveness of our consistency method and the importance of both perspectives. Although either perspective only does not offer good enough results, combining them leads to an excellent result. This observation also meets our assumption that adding more perspectives may further ease the inconsistency problems and resulting in better overall performance. Our method also bears several advantages such as focusing on task and data only, no need for finetuning the LLM part of LVLMs, etc. For details about the advantages of our method and a case study, please refer to Appendix D.

此外，在结合提示I和II后，一致性（I+II）在几乎所有指标上都优于Direct，在大多数指标上也优于一致性(I)和一致性（II），这突出了我们的一致性方法的有效性和这两个观点的重要性。虽然任何一种观点都不能提供足够好的结果，但将它们结合起来可以得到很好的结果。这一观察结果也符合我们的假设，即添加更多的视角可能会进一步缓解不一致的问题，并导致更好的整体性能。我们的方法还具有只关注任务和数据、不需要对LVLM的LLM部分进行微调等优点。关于我们的方法的优点和一个案例研究，请参阅附录D。
